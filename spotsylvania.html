Brought to the United States to be educated as a missionary, Ah Yee was dissatisfied in his adoptive country and wanted to return home to China. At 16 years old, Ah Yee enlisted in the 42nd New York Volunteer Regiment as a corporal. At the battle of Spotsylvania, after all his comrades were killed or wounded, Ah Yee took up the flag, and kept it flying, an act of bravery for which he was unrecognized.
