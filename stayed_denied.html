Found as a half-starved stowaway on a ship named Cohota, Edward worked as a cabin boy before arriving in Gloucester, Massachusetts. In 1864, Cohota joined the 23rd Massachusetts Infantry, where he fought at the Battle of Drewry's Bluff, miraculously surviving after seven shots pierced his clothes. At the Battle of Cold Harbor, Cohota was shot in the head, but despite his injury, he persevered and saved the life of a heavily injured soldier, dragging him to safety before continuing to fight. Cohota received no commendation for his service. After the war, his citizenship was denied due to the Chinese Exclusion Acts.
